Ingredients

  • 200 grams Golden Caster Sugar
  • 200 grams Unsalted Butter (softened plus extra for the tins)
  • 4 Large Eggs
  • 200 grams Self-Raising Flour
  • 7 tablespoons Cocoa Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • ¬Ω teaspoon Vanilla Extract
  • 4 tablespoons Milk
  • 100 grams Milk Chocolate (chopped)
  • 200 grams Butter (softened)
  • 400 grams Icing Sugar
  • 50 grams Dark Chocolate
  • 25 grams Milk Chocolate
  • 25 grams White Chocolate

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180¬∞C (160¬∞C fan) / 350¬∞F / Gas Mark 4.
  2. Grease two 20cm round cake tins with butter and line with baking paper.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the sugar and butter until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, until well combined.
  5. Sift in the flour, cocoa powder, and baking powder, and fold gently into the mixture.
  6. Add the vanilla extract and milk, and mix until smooth.
  7. Fold in the chopped milk chocolate.
  8. Divide the mixture evenly between the two prepared tins.
  9. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Remove from the oven and leave to cool in the tins for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Frosting

  1. Melt the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a pan of simmering water.
  2. Leave to cool slightly.
  3. In a large bowl, beat together the softened butter and icing sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the melted dark chocolate and beat again until well combined.
  5. Spread half of the buttercream over one cake layer, then place the other cake layer on top.
  6. Spread the remaining buttercream over the top and sides of the cake.
  7. Decorate with grated white and milk chocolate.
