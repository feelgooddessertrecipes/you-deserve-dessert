Ingredients

  • 8 slices of bread (white or whole wheat), crusts removed
  • Butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup raisins or sultanas
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350Â°F (180Â°C). Grease a baking dish with butter.
  2. Butter the slices of bread on one side and cut each slice into triangles or smaller pieces.
  3. Arrange half of the bread triangles, buttered side up, in the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle half of the raisins over the bread.
  4. Layer the remaining bread triangles on top and sprinkle the remaining raisins.
  5. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, sugar, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt until well combined.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the bread, ensuring all the bread is soaked. Gently press down on the bread to help it absorb the liquid.
  7. Let it sit for about 15-20 minutes to allow the bread to absorb the custard.
  8. Place the baking dish in a larger roasting pan and fill the roasting pan with hot water until it comes about halfway up the sides of the baking dish. This creates a water bath and helps the pudding cook evenly.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the custard is set. You can test the custard by inserting a knife into the center â€“ if it comes out clean, the pudding is ready.
  10. Once done, remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. You can dust the top with powdered sugar or serve it with a drizzle of custard or whipped cream if desired.
