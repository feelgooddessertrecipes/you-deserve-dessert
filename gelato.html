It sounds like you're interested in making chocolate gelato! Gelato is a rich and creamy Italian frozen dessert similar to ice cream but with a smoother and denser texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, finely chopped
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

1.Prepare the Chocolate Mixture

  • In a medium saucepan, combine the whole milk, heavy cream, and granulated sugar over medium heat. Whisk continuously until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is warmed.

2.Add Cocoa and Chocolate

  • Gradually whisk in the cocoa powder until it is fully incorporated. Add the chopped bittersweet chocolate to the mixture, stirring until the chocolate is melted and the mixture is smooth.

3.Create the Base

  • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Slowly pour a small amount of the chocolate mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs. Once tempered, pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining chocolate mixture, stirring continuously.

4.Cook the Custard

  • Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Be careful not to let it boil.

5.Strain the Mixture

  • Strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any lumps or bits of cooked egg. This will result in a smooth gelato base.

6.Add Vanilla and Salt

  • Stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, combining well.

7.Chill the Mixture

  • Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, making sure the wrap touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.

8.Churn the Gelato

  • Pour the chilled chocolate custard into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.

9.Freeze

  • Transfer the churned gelato into a lidded container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Freeze for an additional 4-6 hours or until the gelato is firm.

10.Serve and Enjoy

  • Scoop the chocolate gelato into bowls or cones and savor the rich, creamy goodness!
