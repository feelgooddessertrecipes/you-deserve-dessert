Certainly! Making homemade jelly is a rewarding and relatively simple process.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of fruit juice (such as grape, apple, or berry)
  • 1/4 cup of lemon juice (for pectin activation)
  • 1 package (1.75 oz) fruit pectin
  • 5 cups of granulated sugar

Instructions

1.Prepare Jars and Lids

  • Wash your canning jars, lids, and bands in hot, soapy water, and rinse well. Sterilize them by placing them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes, or follow the instructions that come with your canning equipment.

2.Prepare Fruit Juice

  • If using fresh fruit, wash and prepare it accordingly. Extract the juice using a fruit juicer or by crushing and straining the fruit. You should have 4 cups of fruit juice.

3.Combine Ingredients

  • In a large pot, combine the fruit juice and lemon juice. Gradually stir in the fruit pectin until it's dissolved.

4.Bring to a Boil

  • Place the pot on the stove over medium-high heat. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil, stirring constantly.

5.Add Sugar

  • Once boiling, add the sugar all at once. Stir well to dissolve the sugar completely.

6.Boil Vigorously

  • Bring the mixture back to a rolling boil. Let it boil vigorously for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.

7.Check Consistency

  • To check if the jelly has reached the desired consistency, perform a gel test. Spoon a small amount onto a cold plate and let it sit for a minute. Push the edge of the jelly with your finger, and if it wrinkles and holds its shape, it's ready.

8.Remove from Heat

  • Once the jelly has reached the desired consistency, remove it from the heat.

9.Skim Foam

  • Skim off any foam that may have formed on the surface of the jelly.

10.Fill Jars

  • Ladle the hot jelly into the prepared jars, leaving about 1/4-inch headspace.

11.Seal Jars

  • Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip-tight.

12.Process Jars

  • Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 5-10 minutes, depending on your altitude. Follow proper canning guidelines for your specific location.

13.Cool and Store

  • Allow the jars to cool completely before checking the seals. Store the sealed jars in a cool, dark place.
