Macarons are a delicate and delicious treat!

Ingredients

For the macaron shells

  • 1 cup (100g) almond flour
  • 1 3/4 cups (200g) powdered sugar
  • 3 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • Food coloring (optional)

For the filling

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (120g) powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a food processor, combine almond flour and powdered sugar. Process until fine and well combined. Sift the mixture into a bowl.
  3. In a clean, dry mixing bowl, beat egg whites on medium speed until foamy. Gradually add granulated sugar while continuing to beat. Increase speed to high and beat until stiff, glossy peaks form.
  4. Gently fold the almond flour mixture into the egg whites using a spatula until the batter is smooth and has a lava-like consistency. If desired, add food coloring at this stage.
  5. Transfer the batter to a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Pipe small circles (about 1 inch in diameter) onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving space between each.
  6. Tap the baking sheets on the counter to release any air bubbles and let the macarons rest for about 30 minutes to form a skin.
  7. Preheat your oven to 300Â°F (150Â°C).
  8. Bake the macarons for 15-18 minutes, or until they have formed feet and are set. Allow them to cool completely on the baking sheets.
  9. For the filling, beat the softened butter until creamy. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and beat until smooth.
  10. Pair up the cooled macaron shells by size. Pipe or spread a small amount of filling onto the flat side of one shell and sandwich it with another.
  11. Refrigerate the filled macarons for at least 24 hours before serving to allow the flavors to meld.
