Srivilliputhur Palkova is a delicious sweet dish that hails from Tamil Nadu, India.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter of full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder
  • A pinch of edible camphor (optional)
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or 1/4 cup of curd

Instructions

  1. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. Once it comes to a boil, add lemon juice or curd to curdle the milk. Stir continuously until the milk solids separate from the whey.
  3. Strain the whey using a muslin cloth or a fine sieve. This leaves you with chenna or paneer.
  4. Now, crumble the chenna and cook it on low heat in the same pan.
  5. Add sugar to the crumbled chenna and continue to cook on low heat.
  6. Stir continuously to avoid burning and ensure that the mixture thickens.
  7. Add cardamom powder and mix well. If you like, you can also add a pinch of edible camphor for flavor or saffron for color.
  8. Keep stirring until the mixture reaches a thick, fudge-like consistency. This process can take some time, so be patient.
  9. Once the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan, turn off the heat.
  10. Allow the Palkova to cool slightly before transferring it to a greased plate or tray.
  11. Let it set for a few hours. Once set, cut it into pieces and enjoy your homemade Srivilliputhur Palkova!
