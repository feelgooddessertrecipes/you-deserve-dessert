Kesari, also known as Sheera or Ravaa Halwa, is a popular Indian sweet dish made from Ravaa (suji), ghee, sugar, and water

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Ravaa (suji)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups water
  • A pinch of saffron strands (kesar), soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnishing (almonds, cashews, pistachios)

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add Ravaa (suji) to the hot ghee and roast it on low to medium flame until it turns golden brown. Keep stirring continuously to avoid burning.
  3. In a separate pan, boil 2 cups of water.
  4. Slowly add the boiling water to the roasted Ravaa while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  5. Add the saffron-soaked milk and mix well. The saffron gives the Kesari a lovely color and aroma.
  6. Stir in sugar and continue cooking on low heat. Keep stirring to prevent lumps and ensure a smooth consistency.
  7. Add cardamom powder for flavor and continue to cook until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  8. Once the Kesari reaches the desired consistency, remove it from heat.
  9. Garnish with chopped nuts of your choice.
