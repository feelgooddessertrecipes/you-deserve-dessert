Adhirasam, a traditional South Indian sweet, is a delightful treat!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1 cup jaggery (grated)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds (optional)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Wash the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4-5 hours.
  2. Drain the water and spread the soaked rice on a cloth to dry for a few hours.
  3. Once the rice is dry, grind it into a fine powder using a mixer or grinder.
  4. In a pan, dissolve the grated jaggery in water over medium heat to make a syrup. Strain it to remove impurities.
  5. Mix the rice flour, cardamom powder, and sesame seeds (if using) in a bowl.
  6. Slowly add the jaggery syrup to the rice flour mixture and knead it into a smooth, thick dough. Make sure there are no lumps.
  7. Let the dough rest for a couple of hours to enhance the flavor.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
  9. Take a small portion of the dough and shape it into a round, flat disc using your hands or a mold.
  10. Carefully slide the shaped adhirasam into the hot oil and fry until it turns golden brown on both sides.
  11. Remove the adhirasam from the oil and place it on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining dough.
