Gulab jamun, the melt-in-your-mouth sweet dumplings, are a delight to make.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • Oil or ghee for frying

For Sugar Syrup

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • A few saffron strands
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, and baking soda. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add milk and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough. Add more milk if needed.
  3. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls without any cracks.
  4. Heat oil or ghee in a pan for deep frying. Ensure the oil is medium-hot.
  5. Fry the jamun balls until they turn golden brown, making sure to rotate them for even cooking. Keep the heat on medium to prevent burning.
  6. Remove the fried jamuns and place them on absorbent paper to drain excess oil.
  7. In a separate pan, prepare the sugar syrup. Combine sugar and water, bring it to a boil, and simmer until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add saffron strands, cardamom powder, and rose water.
  8. Drop the fried jamuns into the warm sugar syrup, ensuring they are fully immersed. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the sweetness.
  9. Serve the gulab jamuns warm or at room temperature. They taste divine when served with a drizzle of syrup.
